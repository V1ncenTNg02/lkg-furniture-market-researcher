EXECUTIVE SUMMARY
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

1. Harvey Norman posted a 6.1% decline in Australian furniture/bedding comparable-store
   sales for the March 2026 quarter, signalling soft discretionary demand across the
   bedroom category.

2. Temple & Webster reported 18% revenue growth for Q3 FY2026, driven by online mattress
   and bedroom furniture demand; online channel shift is accelerating and physical
   specialists are losing share.

3. Forty Winks launched a national 'Sleep Better' promotional campaign (up to 40% off
   mattresses and bases) active through end of May 2026, intensifying promotional
   competition and signalling category-level margin pressure.

4. Adairs reported a 2.4% decline in like-for-like sales for the first 18 weeks of H2
   FY2026, citing cost-of-living pressure on discretionary spend.

5. ABS Retail Trade data (March 2026) shows household goods retailing fell 1.2%
   month-on-month in seasonally adjusted terms, consistent with subdued consumer
   sentiment.

6. Nick Scali flagged an 8% increase in the written sales order book for April 2026,
   suggesting a potential near-term demand recovery in higher-end furniture.

7. IKEA Australia announced a new 10,000 sqm store opening in Marsden Park (western
   Sydney) for Q3 2026, expanding bedroom and sleep-product competition in NSW and
   increasing competitive density in a major metro catchment.

8. Snooze / Hypnos: no material public announcements identified in the review period;
   website reflects ongoing promotional positioning with finance offers.

9. AUD/USD at approximately 0.64 as of early May 2026 creates ongoing cost pressure on
   imported mattress components and bedroom furniture; margin headwind persists.

10. Two items (Temple & Webster growth rate and Nick Scali order book) carry human-review
    flags and should be verified against primary ASX documents before use in board
    materials.

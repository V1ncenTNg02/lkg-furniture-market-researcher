## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

The Australian mattress and bedroom furniture market (IBISWorld 2026 size: AUD 4.4 billion) is navigating a period of measured recovery following the inflation and interest-rate headwinds of 2022-2024. Consumer confidence is stabilising but spending remains selective, with value-signalling promotions dominating the competitive landscape this week. Key dynamics for LKG / Hypnos Group to monitor:

1. **Snooze (owned by Hypnos Group)** is running a live Member Event closing 14 May 2026, with targeted member-exclusive discounts across mattresses, linen, and bedroom suites. A parallel Protect-A-Bed Sensation Bamboo protector offer closes 10 May 2026. These are time-critical activations requiring store-level execution focus.

2. **Forty Winks** is running its "Forty Frenzy" sale — a broad promotional event covering premium mattress brands (Sealy, King Koil, TEMPUR, Silent Partner, Mlily) and bedroom furniture — directly competing with Snooze's Member Event window.

3. **Temple & Webster (ASX:TPW)** reported H1 FY26 revenue of AUD 376 million (+20% YoY) with record 1.35 million active customers, demonstrating that the pure-play online channel continues to take structural share. The online segment is a growing indirect threat to bricks-and-mortar sleep specialists.

4. **Adairs (ASX:ADH)** delivered 1H FY26 group sales of AUD 329 million (+5.9% YoY), with its Mocka brand surging 29.8% and online Adairs sales up 13.5%. Focus on Furniture, however, showed like-for-like sales of -3.3%, a signal that physical furniture formats face ongoing pressure — directly relevant to G&G Furniture positioning.

5. **Nick Scali (ASX:NCK)** reported ANZ revenue of AUD 251.7 million (+13.1% YoY) in H1 FY26 with gross margins expanding 150 basis points to 65.9%, demonstrating that premium furniture retail with strong brand identity and disciplined pricing can deliver superior margins.

6. **Harvey Norman (ASX:HVN)** posted H1 FY26 net income of AUD 321.9 million (+47% YoY underlying), though the share price has declined 33.8% YTD to AUD 4.42 as of 6 May 2026, reflecting investor concern over property revaluation gains (>20% of profit) and macro exposure.

7. **IKEA Australia** tightened its returns policy effective 9 April 2026, reducing the change-of-mind window for assembled products from 365 days to 60 days — a competitive policy change that may shift some risk-averse consumers toward competitors offering more flexible terms.

8. The **ABS Retail Trade series ceased publication** after June 2025 (final release 31 July 2025). Household goods data from July 2025 onwards is available only through the Monthly Household Spending Indicator (MHSI). The last published furniture, floor coverings, houseware and textile goods figure was +3.0% month-on-month in June 2025.

**Overall Market Read:** The sector is in a slow-growth, high-promotion environment. Physical specialists must compete on service, trial experiences, and loyalty mechanics. The LKG / Hypnos Group franchise network faces twin pressures: online channel growth (Temple & Webster, Mocka) and margin compression at the furniture-focused end (Focus on Furniture comp -3.3%). The current Snooze Member Event is well-timed but must be supported with strong in-store conversion to offset the Forty Frenzy competitive event running concurrently.

## 1. INDUSTRY OVERVIEW

### Market Size and Structure
- **Total industry revenue (2026):** AUD 4.4 billion (IBISWorld, Mattress and Bedroom Furniture Retailing, 2026)
  - [CONFIDENCE: Medium — IBISWorld estimate; not independently cross-verified against primary ABS data for 2026 given ABS Retail Trade discontinuation]
- **5-year revenue CAGR (2021-2026):** 1.0% annualised (IBISWorld)
- **Business count:** 1,142 businesses operating in the industry (2026, IBISWorld), growing at 0.4% CAGR 2021-2026
- **Competition:** Characterised as "high and increasing" (IBISWorld)

### Value Chain
The industry spans: mattress/bedding manufacturers (domestic and import) → wholesale distributors → specialty retailers (Snooze, Forty Winks, Bedshed, Sleeping Giant) → department/general furniture retailers (Harvey Norman, Nick Scali, Amart) → online pure-plays (Temple & Webster, Mocka) → consumer.

Hypnos Group operates primarily in specialty retail through Snooze (franchise network) and Future Sleep, and in mid-market furniture through G&G Furniture.

### Demand Drivers
- **Housing turnover and new dwelling formation:** Dwelling turnover drives replacement cycles for mattresses and bedroom furniture. RBA rate pause in 2025-26 has stabilised property transactions but volumes remain below 2021 peak.
- **Consumer confidence:** Selective recovery following 2022-24 cost-of-living squeeze. Value-seeking behaviour remains pronounced.
- **Product replacement cycles:** Average mattress replacement cycle 8-10 years. Stimulus-pulled forward demand in 2020-21 (sector grew 7.9% that year) is now a multi-year headwind as replacement cohort is smaller.
- **Online channel penetration:** Structurally increasing. Temple & Webster's 20% revenue growth and 1.35 million active customers demonstrate continued digital adoption.
- **Sleep health awareness:** Growing consumer engagement with sleep quality, driving premiumisation in the mattress sub-category (hybrid, smart beds, health-positioned products).

### Why Now (May 2026)
- The sector is entering a key promotional window: Mother's Day (11 May 2026), followed by the mid-year EOFY sale season (June). Competitors are activating major promotional events now. Snooze's Member Event closes 14 May, directly overlapping with Forty Winks' Forty Frenzy.
- IKEA's returns policy tightening (April 2026) creates a potential conversion opportunity for competitors with more flexible trial-and-return terms.
- The ABS Retail Trade data discontinuation means industry participants must now use MHSI for macro demand signals — a methodology change that requires recalibration of internal benchmarking tools.

# LKG Research Plan

description: Research plan for Australian bedding, mattresses, sleep products, and bedroom furniture market monitoring. Use for weekly or daily market scans, competitor watch, demand indicators, and source gathering.

## Objective

Find public market signals that may matter to LKG, Hypnos Group, Snooze, Future Sleep, G&G Furniture, portfolio company GMs, or the LKG board.

## Default Scope

- Geography: Australia.
- Time window: last 7 days unless the user specifies otherwise.
- Sector: bedding, mattresses, sleep products, bedroom furniture, and adjacent home retail.
- Output: structured research findings, not a final digest.

## Source Priorities

Use public sources only:

1. Official company investor pages and ASX announcements.
2. ABS retail trade and other public economic datasets.
3. Company websites for promotions, store changes, product launches, delivery offers, and financing offers.
4. Reputable business, retail, property, and consumer news.
5. Public annual reports, trading updates, and investor presentations.

Avoid or flag:

- Unsourced social posts.
- Paywalled snippets without enough public evidence.
- Broker research unless publicly accessible.
- Any confidential or employer-provided data.

## Research Tasks

For each run, search for:

- Competitor promotions and pricing changes.
- Product launches and range changes.
- Store openings, closures, refurbishments, or franchise changes.
- Delivery, financing, warranty, or service offer changes.
- Demand signals from retail trade, housing activity, consumer confidence, and discretionary spending.
- Supply chain and margin signals: freight, FX, raw materials, importing, manufacturing, and inventory.
- Strategic signals: M&A, major partnerships, regulatory changes, reputation risk, or sector consolidation.

## Rules

- Cite every factual claim with a URL.
- If evidence is not strong enough, lower confidence or flag for review.
- Separate factual summary from business interpretation.
- Do not recommend distribution to GMs or the board. Leave routing to the classifier.
